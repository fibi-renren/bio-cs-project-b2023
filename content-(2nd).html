    The Cell Organelles

  • Plasma Membrane
    • cell's outer covering
    • separates the cell from other cells and the environment
    • controls what materials go in and out of the cell
  • Nucleus
    • in charge of the cellular activities
    • contains information that will tell the cell what to do, what to make, and when to divide
  • Ribosomes
    • this is where proteins are produces or synthesized that is needed for many cell functions such as damage repairing
    • translates RNA into protein
  • Smooth Endoplasmic Reticulum
    • synthesizes carbohydrates and lipids
    • carbohydrate metabolism
  • Rough Endoplasmic Reticulum
    • defining feature is the ribosomes attached to it
    • synthesizes proteins and packages/folds lipids
    • transports proteins and lipids packaged in vesicles by the RER
  • Golgi Apparatus
    • helps sort out the proteins synthesized in the RER
    • proteins transported here are may be stored or chemically modified then packaged for delivery to the cell membrane
    • transports proteins and lipids packaged in vesicles by the RER
  • Lysosome
    • breaks down macromolecules such as nucleic acids, proteins, and polysaccharides
    • destroys obsolete components inside the cell
  • Peroxisome
    • contains enzymes that oxidize certain molecules found in the cell, notably fatty acids and amino acids which produces hydrogen peroxide
  • Vacuole
    • commonly smaller in animal cells but they are usually larger in plant cells
    • used to store materials or transport substances
  • Mitochondrion
    • site of cellular respiration
    • produces ATP which is the energy form the cell uses
